## Main Discussion

The core idea is simple: if you could hand your teammates one capability to make Fabric and Power BI more usable tomorrow, what would it be? The "gifts" end up clustering around two themes: **(1) visibility and governance** for admins and platform owners, and **(2) better understanding and workflow support** for report builders and model authors.

Key takeaways:

- **Oversight needs to be faster**: a "God mode" view of Fabric artifacts (by domain, workspace, and security group) would remove a ton of click-hunting.
- **Lineage has to catch silent failures**: beyond ‘pipeline failed’, teams need signals when data quality or business logic drifts while everything still *technically* refreshes.
- **Cost and billback must be first-class**: tying capacity and CU usage back to workspaces, departments, and artifacts is what makes governance stick.
- **Lower the semantic-model learning curve**: showing a graphical view of what DAX / visuals are doing behind the scenes would accelerate adoption for new builders.
- **Migration shouldn’t mean rewriting**: importing/translating existing pipelines and notebooks (Databricks, Synapse, ADF, etc.) into Fabric would remove a major blocker.
- **Region alignment is not optional**: co-locating Fabric, Power BI, and data reduces cost (especially with shortcuts) and avoids performance surprises.
- **AI has to be contextual and affordable**: Copilot-style help is only useful when it understands the artifact you’re editing (and doesn’t require a giant licensing leap).
- **Pushback workflows matter**: getting insights back into source systems (‘reflection’) is part of making BI actionable, not just descriptive.

## Looking Forward

The more Fabric grows, the more these "boring" platform features—governance, monitoring, cost attribution, and clarity for builders—will decide whether teams scale successfully or stall out.
